# 🦉 mirowl: Your Intelligent Visual Memory

**Google-like search for your screenshots, local-first AI for your eyes.**

mirowl is a privacy-first AI assistant that transforms your messy folders of screenshots and images into a searchable, organized knowledge base. Using state-of-the-art Local OCR and AI categorization, mirowl allows you to find any image by the text inside it or the context of its content—all without your data ever leaving your machine.

## 🚀 Download & Installation

1. Go to the [Releases Page](../../releases/latest).
2. **For macOS:** Download `mirowl_X.X.X_universal.dmg` (Works for both Apple Silicon and Intel).
   - Open the `.dmg` and drag **mirowl** to your Applications folder.
   - mirowl is **Apple-Notarized**, so it will run immediately without security warnings.
3. Launch mirowl. It will guide you through a 1-minute onboarding to select your "Watch Folders" (like Desktop or Downloads).

## ✨ Why mirowl? (The Intelligence Promise)

Most "AI" tools upload your screenshots to the cloud. mirowl is different. We believe your visual memory—your bank details, private messages, and unreleased designs—should stay on your disk.

- **🚫 We Never:** Upload your images to a server, train models on your data, or require an internet connection for core search.
- **✅ We Always:** Process OCR locally on your GPU/NPU, encrypt your local database, and keep your visual history 100% private.

### Core Capabilities

- **Deep Text Search (OCR):** Find a specific line of code, a receipt number, or a quote hidden inside a screenshot taken months ago.
- **AI Auto-Categorization:** Automatically groups your images into "UI Design," "Code Snippets," "Invoices," and "Social Media" based on visual context.
- **Unclutter Logic:** Automatically moves old screenshots from your messy Desktop into organized, searchable library folders.
- **Universal Drag-and-Drop:** Found the image you need? Drag it directly from mirowl into Slack, Discord, or Figma instantly.
- **Source Context:** mirowl remembers _where_ you were. Search by the app you were using (e.g., "Find screenshots from Xcode").
